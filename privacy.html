🍜 Noodle — Privacy Policy

Your 10-second silly break. Here's the boring-but-important part.
Last updated: July 13, 2026

Noodle is a micro-break app made by Celeste Torres, operating under the name Loafing Labs. This page explains, plainly, what information Noodle collects, why, and what your options are. We kept the jokes out of this one on purpose — you deserve a straight answer here, not a bit.

The short version

Noodle does not have user accounts, does not show ads, and does not sell your data — ever. We collect a small amount of technical information to make the app work and to understand, in aggregate, how it's used. None of it is linked to your identity, and none of it is used to track you across other apps or websites.

What we collect

Data typeWhat it isWhy we collect it
Purchase History Records of your Noodle Pro subscription purchase, handled through Apple's App Store To unlock Pro features and manage your subscription
Device ID An anonymous identifier for your device (not your name, email, or Apple ID) To enforce fair-use limits (like daily AI content caps), keep your favorites and seen-content list working, and verify Pro status
Product Interaction Basic, anonymous usage data — e.g. that a button was tapped To understand which parts of Noodle are working well and which need improvement

None of this data is linked to your identity, and none of it is used to track you. Apple's App Privacy label for Noodle reflects this: "Data Not Linked to You."

Who we share it with

We use a small number of trusted service providers to run Noodle. Each only receives the minimum data needed to do its job, and each is contractually and technically required to protect that data to at least the same standard described in this policy:

  • RevenueCat — manages subscription purchases and Pro status.
  • TelemetryDeck — provides anonymous, privacy-first usage analytics. TelemetryDeck is designed from the ground up to avoid collecting personal data.
  • Supabase — hosts Noodle's content library and a server-side function that enforces the daily AI-content limit for Pro users.
  • hCaptcha — runs a one-time, anonymous bot-check when the app is first opened, to protect our systems from abuse.

We do not share data with advertisers, and we do not use any of the above services for cross-app tracking.

AI-generated content

Pro subscribers may occasionally receive content generated by an AI system (Anthropic's Claude, with OpenAI's GPT-4o mini as a backup). Generating this content does not involve sending any personal data — only the content type and a short list of previously shown items, so the AI avoids repeating itself. No user data is used to train any AI model.

How long we keep it

Most of Noodle's data — your favorites, your seen-content history, your settings — lives locally on your device, not on our servers. It stays there until you delete the app. The anonymous device ID and related usage data held by our service providers is retained only as long as needed for the purposes above, and is never linked back to you as a person.

Your choices

  • Delete local data: Uninstalling Noodle removes your favorites, seen-content list, and settings from your device.
  • Export favorites (Pro): Pro subscribers can manually export and restore their favorites at any time via Settings.
  • Manage your subscription: Handled entirely through your Apple ID account settings.
  • Ask us anything: If you have questions about data tied to your device, or want to request its deletion from our service providers, just email us — see below.

Children's privacy

Noodle is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. Since Noodle has no accounts, sign-up, or personal data collection of any kind, there is nothing for a child (or anyone) to hand over in the first place.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, we'll update the date at the top of this page. We won't make changes that reduce your protections without making that clear.
